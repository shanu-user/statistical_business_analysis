HYPOTHESIS TEST RESULTS

--------------------------------------------------
1. CORRELATION TEST: Quantity vs Total Sales
--------------------------------------------------

Null Hypothesis (H0):
There is no relationship between Quantity and Total Sales.

Alternative Hypothesis (H1):
There is a significant relationship between Quantity and Total Sales.

Results:
Correlation Coefficient (r): 0.688
p-value: 2.58e-15

Decision:
Since p-value < 0.05, we reject the null hypothesis.

Conclusion:
There is a strong positive and statistically significant relationship 
between Quantity and Total Sales. Increasing quantity leads to higher revenue.


--------------------------------------------------
2. CORRELATION TEST: Price vs Total Sales
--------------------------------------------------

Null Hypothesis (H0):
There is no relationship between Price and Total Sales.

Alternative Hypothesis (H1):
There is a significant relationship between Price and Total Sales.

Results:
Correlation Coefficient (r): 0.646
p-value: 3.88e-13

Decision:
Since p-value < 0.05, we reject the null hypothesis.

Conclusion:
There is a moderate to strong positive and statistically significant 
relationship between Price and Total Sales.


--------------------------------------------------
3. ANOVA TEST: Product vs Total Sales
--------------------------------------------------

Null Hypothesis (H0):
There is no significant difference in Total Sales across different products.

Alternative Hypothesis (H1):
At least one product has significantly different Total Sales.

Results:
F-statistic: 2.05
p-value: 0.093

Decision:
Since p-value > 0.05, we fail to reject the null hypothesis.

Conclusion:
There is no statistically significant difference in Total Sales 
between different product categories. Product type does not significantly 
impact sales performance.


--------------------------------------------------
OVERALL INSIGHT
--------------------------------------------------

Total Sales are significantly influenced by Quantity and Price, 
while Product category does not show a statistically significant impact.

The primary drivers of revenue are:
1. Quantity (strongest influence)
2. Price (moderate influence)
